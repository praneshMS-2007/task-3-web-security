# 05 — Reporting & Remediation

## Why Reporting Matters

A penetration test is incomplete without a clear report that explains the findings, their impact, and how to remediate them.

## Typical Report Structure

1. Executive summary
2. Scope and methodology
3. Findings and evidence
4. Risk ratings
5. Recommendations and remediation steps
6. Conclusion

## Example Risk Ratings

- Critical — immediate threat, severe impact
- High — important issue, significant exposure
- Medium — moderate risk, fix in a planned cycle
- Low — minor issue, lower urgency

## Remediation Recommendations

- Patch vulnerable software
- Remove default credentials
- Apply least privilege policies
- Harden web and system configurations
- Enable logging and monitoring

## Final Note

The goal of testing is not only to find flaws but also to help the organization improve its security posture.
