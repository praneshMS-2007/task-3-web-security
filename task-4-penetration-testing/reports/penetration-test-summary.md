# Penetration Testing Summary Report

## Scope

This report summarizes the key phases of a penetration test: reconnaissance, vulnerability discovery, exploitation, privilege escalation, and reporting.

## Findings Summary

- Open services and ports were identified during reconnaissance.
- Potential misconfigurations and vulnerabilities were documented.
- Exploitation was described at a conceptual and controlled level.
- Privilege escalation and post-exploitation considerations were included.
- Recommendations focus on remediation and hardening.

## Risk Overview

| Severity | Summary |
|----------|---------|
| Critical | High-impact issues requiring immediate remediation |
| High | Significant weaknesses with practical exploit potential |
| Medium | Moderate issues that should be addressed soon |
| Low | Minor findings and hardening opportunities |

## Recommendations

1. Patch systems and services regularly.
2. Restrict unnecessary access and privileges.
3. Use secure configurations and monitoring.
4. Maintain logs and incident response readiness.
5. Document and retest remediation work.
